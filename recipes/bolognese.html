Mom's Spaghetti Bolognese

Description

Spaghetti Bolognese is one of the most popular pasta dishes worldwide, but interestingly, it is not an authentic Italian dish—at least, not in the way most people think!

The dish is inspired by Ragù alla Bolognese, a traditional meat sauce from Bologna, Italy. However, in Italy, this sauce is never served with spaghetti! Instead, it is typically paired with tagliatelle (a flat, ribbon-like pasta) because the sauce clings better to its surface.

Ingredients

  • 1 package spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 stalk celery, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 3 gloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 cans crushed tomatoes
  • 2 teaspoons white sugar
  • salt and ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
  • 1/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan Cheese

Steps

  1. The dish is inspired by Ragù alla Bolognese, a traditional meat sauce from Bologna, Italy. However, in Italy, this sauce is never served with spaghetti! Instead, it is typically paired with tagliatelle (a flat, ribbon-like pasta) because the sauce clings better to its surface. Stir in spaghetti and return to a boil. Cook spaghetti uncovered, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 12 minutes; drain.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat for 8 to 10 minutes. Add onion, celery, carrot, and oregano; continue cooking until vegetables begin to soften, 8 to 10 minutes. Add garlic; cook until fragrant, about 2 minutes. Crumble in ground beef; cook and stir until no longer pink and completely cooked, 8 to 10 minutes.
  3. Pour vinegar over ground beef mixture; simmer until liquid evaporates, about 5 minutes. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and sugar; bring to a boil, season with salt and black pepper, then remove from heat. Stir in basil.
  4. Spoon sauce over spaghetti; top with Parmesan cheese to serve.
