Turkish Doner Kebab

Description

Doner Kebab is a popular Turkish dish made from seasoned meat (usually lamb, beef, or chicken) stacked on a vertical rotisserie. As the meat cooks, thin slices are shaved off and served in flatbreads, pita, wraps, or with rice and salad.

Although the meal is originally Turkish, it is said, that the dish was first founded in the German capital Berlin by a Turkish immigrant. Hence why the capital's most famous fast food is the Doner, and the German populus agreeing that the best and most original Doner is found there.

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried Italian herb seasoning
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 1/4 pounds ground beef
  • Flat white bread
  • Vegetables of your choice

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Combine flour, oregano, salt, garlic powder, onion powder, Italian seasoning, black pepper, and cayenne pepper in a large bowl. Add ground lamb and knead until thoroughly mixed together, about 3 minutes.
  3. Shape seasoned lamb mixture and place into a loaf pan; set on top of a baking sheet.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven, turning halfway to ensure even browning, for about 1 hour and 20 minutes.
  5. Wrap loaf in aluminum foil and let rest for about 10 minutes. Slice as thinly as possible to make doner kebab pieces.
  6. Assemble your Doner with the doner slices and your favorite vegetables inside of a flat white bread.
